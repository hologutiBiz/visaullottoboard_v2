Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE) Safety Standards

Last updated: July 2025

App Context

Visual Lotto Board is a suite of tools designed to help users analyze and classify lotto numbers. While our apps do not include social networking, messaging, or user-generated content, we maintain a zero-tolerance policy toward any form of child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE). We are committed to upholding safety standards and complying with Google Play’s CSAE policy.

Zero-Tolerance Policy

Visual Lotto Board maintains a zero-tolerance policy toward any form of child sexual abuse and exploitation. We are committed to creating a safe and respectful environment for all users.

Definitions

CSAE includes any content, behavior, or communication that sexualizes minors, promotes exploitation, or facilitates grooming. We align our definitions with international standards including those from UNICEF and INTERPOL.

Prohibited Content and Behavior

  • Sexualization or exploitation of minors
  • Grooming or solicitation of underage users
  • Sharing, uploading, or linking to CSAE-related media

Prevention Measures

  • Age verification and user screening (where applicable)
  • Manual and automated content moderation (if future features require)
  • Strict community guidelines and enforcement

Detection and Reporting

Should any CSAE-related content or behavior be detected, our team will take immediate action. Reports can be submitted via our support email, and serious violations will be escalated to relevant authorities, including the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC).

Response Procedures

  • Immediate removal of violating content
  • Suspension or permanent banning of offending accounts
  • Preservation of evidence for legal investigation

Support and Resources

If you encounter CSAE-related content or behavior, please contact us immediately at support@visuallottoboard.com.

We also encourage users to report directly to child protection organizations such as CyberTipline.

Compliance

We regularly audit our safety policies and update them to remain compliant with Google Play’s CSAE standards and international child protection laws.
